Taj Mahal in Mixed Reality

Best FYP Award
Best FYP Award

3D Taj Mahal Model: The application features a detailed 3D model of the Taj Mahal, created using software tools like Blender. Users can explore the Taj Mahal from various angles and appreciate its intricate architecture.

Interactive AR Marker: Users can scan an AR marker to bring a virtual Taj Mahal model to life. This feature allows users to interact with the Taj Mahal and learn more about it through augmented reality.

Educational Videos: Within the history and architecture scenes, users can access informative videos that provide in-depth insights into the Taj Mahal's historical significance and architectural details.

Interactive Elements: The application includes interactive elements that allow users to manipulate the Taj Mahal model, such as rotating it for a 360-degree view and peeling back layers to explore its interior.

Coloring Scene: A unique feature lets users color the Taj Mahal using a color palette and sliders, allowing for creative customization of the monument's appearance.

Photo Gallery: Users can browse a stunning photo gallery showcasing the Taj Mahal's beauty from different angles and lighting conditions.

Multiplayer Interaction: In a multiplayer scene, users can collaboratively interact with the Taj Mahal, fostering a shared learning experience.

Eye Tracking: Eye-tracking technology allows users to interact with holograms across their field of view quickly and effortlessly. It also makes the system smarter by better identifying the user's intentions.

Quiz Through Browser: Implementing a quiz through a web browser in mixed reality application for the Taj Mahal on HoloLens 2 is a way to engage users and provide them with an interactive educational experience.

Technologies Used

C# Unity HoloLens2

HealthLearn (Skeleton Exploration & Heart Clipping)

Interactive Skeleton: In the center of the room, you'll find a life-sized, three-dimensional model of the human skeleton. This model is detailed and accurate, offering a realistic representation of the bones in the human body.

Part-by-Part Interaction: Using intuitive gestures or voice commands, you can select and interact with individual bones. Simply point, tap, or speak to learn about each bone's name, location, and function.

Educational Insights: As you explore, informative labels and descriptions will appear alongside the bones you select. A friendly, virtual guide will provide explanations, making learning engaging and enjoyable.

Full Control: You have full control over your exploration. Rotate, zoom in, or pan to get a closer look at specific bones. Take your time to absorb the knowledge at your own pace.

Heart Clipping: Users can dissect a virtual heart using clipping planes, spheres, and cubes, revealing its internal structuresThis 3D visual representation will help them understand and visualize the heart in great detail.
